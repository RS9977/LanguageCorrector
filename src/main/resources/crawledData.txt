<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa" class="mp-contains-float"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;">
<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 114px;">
<span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:Bust_of_Sulla_(loan_from_Ny_Carlsberg_Glyptotek)_-_Glyptothek_-_Munich_-_Germany_2017.jpg" class="mw-file-description" title="Marble bust of Scipio, 2nd century&#160;BC"><img alt="Marble bust of Scipio, 2nd century&#160;BC" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Bust_of_Sulla_%28loan_from_Ny_Carlsberg_Glyptotek%29_-_Glyptothek_-_Munich_-_Germany_2017.jpg/114px-Bust_of_Sulla_%28loan_from_Ny_Carlsberg_Glyptotek%29_-_Glyptothek_-_Munich_-_Germany_2017.jpg" decoding="async" width="114" height="171" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Bust_of_Sulla_%28loan_from_Ny_Carlsberg_Glyptotek%29_-_Glyptothek_-_Munich_-_Germany_2017.jpg/171px-Bust_of_Sulla_%28loan_from_Ny_Carlsberg_Glyptotek%29_-_Glyptothek_-_Munich_-_Germany_2017.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a6/Bust_of_Sulla_%28loan_from_Ny_Carlsberg_Glyptotek%29_-_Glyptothek_-_Munich_-_Germany_2017.jpg/228px-Bust_of_Sulla_%28loan_from_Ny_Carlsberg_Glyptotek%29_-_Glyptothek_-_Munich_-_Germany_2017.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3456" data-file-height="5184" /></a></span><div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Marble bust of Scipio, 2nd century&#160;BC</div></div>
</div>
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_New_Carthage" title="Battle of New Carthage">battle of New Carthage</a></b>, part of the <a href="/wiki/Second_Punic_War" title="Second Punic War">Second Punic War</a>, took place in early 209&#160;BC when a <a href="/wiki/Roman_Republic" title="Roman Republic">Roman</a> army under <a href="/wiki/Scipio_Africanus" title="Scipio Africanus">Publius Scipio</a> <i>(bust pictured)</i> assaulted <a href="/wiki/Cartagena,_Spain" title="Cartagena, Spain">New Carthage</a>, held by a <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Carthage" title="Ancient Carthage">Carthaginian</a> garrison under Mago. Late in 210&#160;BC Scipio took command of Roman forces in Iberia (modern Spain and Portugal) and decided to strike at the regional centre of Carthaginian power: its capital, New Carthage. He marched on the city and immediately attacked it. After defeating a Carthaginian force outside the <a href="/wiki/Defensive_wall" title="Defensive wall">walls</a>, he pressed attacks on the east <a href="/wiki/Gate" title="Gate">gate</a> and the walls. Both were repulsed, but later that day Scipio renewed them. Hard-pressed, Mago moved men from the north wall, overlooking a broad, shallow <a href="/wiki/Lagoon" title="Lagoon">lagoon</a>. Anticipating this, a force of 500 men waded the lagoon to scale the north wall unopposed. They fought their way to the east gate, opened it from inside and let in their comrades. The city fell and became a logistics centre for the Roman war effort. By 206&#160;BC the Carthaginians had been expelled from Iberia. (<b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_New_Carthage" title="Battle of New Carthage">Full&#160;article...</a></b>)
</p>

<h2 id="mp-tfp-h2" class="mp-h2"><span id="Today.27s_featured_picture"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Today's_featured_picture">Today's featured picture</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfp">
<table role="presentation" style="margin:0 3px 3px; width:100%; box-sizing:border-box; text-align:center; background-color:transparent; border-collapse:collapse; padding:0.9em">
<tbody><tr>
<td><span typeof="mw:File"><a href="/wiki/File:1581_Bunting_clover_leaf_map.jpg" class="mw-file-description" title="Bünting cloverleaf map"><img alt="Bünting cloverleaf map" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/1581_Bunting_clover_leaf_map.jpg/600px-1581_Bunting_clover_leaf_map.jpg" decoding="async" width="600" height="472" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/1581_Bunting_clover_leaf_map.jpg/900px-1581_Bunting_clover_leaf_map.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/1581_Bunting_clover_leaf_map.jpg/1200px-1581_Bunting_clover_leaf_map.jpg 2x" data-file-width="4441" data-file-height="3496" /></a></span>
</td></tr>
<tr>
<td style="padding:0 0.9em; text-align:left;">
<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/B%C3%BCnting_cloverleaf_map" title="Bünting cloverleaf map">Bünting cloverleaf map</a></b> is a historic <a href="/wiki/Mappa_mundi" title="Mappa mundi">mappa mundi</a> drawn by the German theologian and cartographer <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_B%C3%BCnting" title="Heinrich Bünting">Heinrich Bünting</a>. The map was published in his book <span title="Latin-language text"><i lang="la">Itinerarium Sacrae Scripturae</i></span> in 1581. The map depicts the three continents of the Old World, Europe, Africa and Asia, as three leaves forming the shape of a <a href="/wiki/Clover" title="Clover">clover</a>, with <a href="/wiki/Jerusalem" title="Jerusalem">Jerusalem</a> at the centre. The three continents include captions for some of their countries and illustrations of cities. The clover is surrounded by the <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">ocean</a>, with its surface including illustrations of sea creatures, monsters, and a ship. England and Denmark are represented as two island-shapes above Europe's leaf, while the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a> are shown as a mostly unrevealed shape in the lower left corner, captioned <span title="German-language text"><i lang="de">Die Neue Welt</i></span> (the <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">New World</a>).
</p>
